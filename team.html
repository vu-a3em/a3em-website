Team & Partners

A3EM is a collaboration between engineers at Vanderbilt University and ecologists at Colorado State University, working alongside wildlife agencies and field researchers around the world.

Collaborators

Partner organizations

Field deployments depend on close collaboration with wildlife agencies, research stations, and technology manufacturers around the world.

Colorado State University

Academic partner institution; collaborative ecological research, NSF-funded.

Colorado Parks & Wildlife

Bighorn sheep pneumonia-detection deployments, Fort Collins facility.

Navajo Nation

Wild bighorn sheep deployments in Utah and Arizona, under a data-ownership agreement with the project.

Large Animal Research Station (University of Alaska Fairbanks)

Captive caribou deployments studying insect harassment, Fairbanks, Alaska.

Save The Elephants

Wild savanna elephant deployments and GPS-collar integration, Samburu National Reserve, Kenya.

World Wildlife Fund

First-ever forest elephant audiologger deployments, Central African Republic.

Scandinavian Brown Bear Research Project (SBRRP)

New brown bear collaboration alongside an existing GPS-collar program, Sweden.

Osa Conservation

New white-lipped peccary collaboration for a year-long GPS-collar study, Costa Rica.

Savannah Tracking Ltd.

Kenyan wildlife-tracking device manufacturer; ongoing technology-transfer dialogue and collar/drop-off integration.

Telonics, Inc.

Supplier of the drop-off mechanisms now standardized on for critical elephant deployments.

Kasetsart University, Bangkok

Partner on the Thailand acoustic-monitoring workshop; formal MOU signed with CSU.

WildLabs

Global conservation-technology network; hosted an invited project tech talk and funded a companion proposal.

A3EM

Animal-Borne Adaptive Acoustic Environmental Monitoring — an NSF-sponsored Cyber-Physical Systems project developing real-time, adaptive acoustic sensing for wildlife research and conservation.
